The system separates your "Class" (determined by your weapon) from your "Magic" (determined by your race). To maximize your efficiency, you must invest points into both trees simultaneously. <Callout type="tip" title="Focus Your Points">

The **grand alfheim skill tree** for classes is highly dependent on your primary attribute, usually Strength (STR) or Dexterity (DEX).

Choosing the right class tree is the first step in defining your role. If you prefer solo play, the Swordsman or Bowmaster trees offer the most versatility. For group-oriented players, the Aegis and Undine (Support) trees are essential for clearing endgame dungeons like Ode to the Abyss.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Racial Magic Trees and Elemental Synergy">

Unlike class skills, your magic tree is permanent to your race. Each of the nine races in Grand Alfheim starts with a unique elemental affinity. These magic trees provide utility that often covers the weaknesses of your class. For instance, a Gnome's Earth Magic can provide the durability a glass-cannon Assassin might lack.

**Racial Magic Breakdown:**
- **Salamander (Fire):** Pure offensive pressure and burning effects.
- **Sylph (Wind):** Enhanced movement speed and aerial repositioning.
- **Undine (Water):** Critical healing and sustain for party play.
- **Cait Sith (Summoning):** Beast-taming and companion-based utility.
- **Spriggan (Illusion):** Deception and treasure-finding capabilities.
- **Pooka (Music):** Buffing allies and disrupting enemy rhythms.
- **Gnome (Earth):** Frontline durability and defensive constructs.
- **Imp (Curse):** Debuffs and enhanced nighttime vision.
- **Leprechaun (Lightning):** Crafting-themed utility and mechanical assistance.

<Callout type="info" title="Magic Progression">
Magic skills level up as you use them in combat. Unlike class skills which require points, magic proficiency often grows through active participation in battles, making it a natural part of your leveling journey.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: How to Optimize Your Skill Tree">

Navigating the **grand alfheim skill tree** requires a methodical approach to ensure you don't hit a progression wall at level 20. Follow these steps to build a character capable of tackling the Bone Dragon and beyond.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Core Class">
    Visit the forge in the starting territory. Pick a weapon that feels comfortable. The Swordsman is the most recommended for beginners due to its balanced skill tree.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Allocate Initial Stat Points">
    As you level from 1-10, put points into your primary damage stat (STR or DEX). Do not ignore Stamina; your skill tree abilities consume stamina, and running out during a combo leaves you vulnerable.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Unlock Active Skills First">
    Prioritize active skills (combat moves) over passive stat boosts in the early stages of the tree. Having more tools in your kit allows for better reaction to different enemy types.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Integrate Racial Magic">
    By level 15, you should have at least two magic abilities unlocked. Practice "weaving" these between weapon attacks to maximize your damage-per-second (DPS).
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Refine for Endgame">
    Upon reaching level 50, visit the blacksmith to refine your build. Focus on skills that specifically help with the mechanics of the Ode to the Abyss dungeon, such as high burst for the tower phase.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Dungeon Readiness">
Before entering the Temple of Beginnings, ensure your skill tree includes at least one mobility skill (like a dash or lunge) to avoid the Bone Dragon's area-of-effect attacks.
</Callout>

</Section>
