Once you enter the **grand alfheim temple of beginnings location**, you are presented with a choice that dictates your experience for the next 15–20 minutes. The dungeon is split into two distinct wings that converge at the central puzzle chamber.

Regardless of the path chosen, you will eventually reach the altar rooms. These rooms serve as a gatekeeper mechanic, preventing players from simply rushing to the boss without engaging with the dungeon's environment.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Clear the Initial Wing">
    Navigate through your chosen path (Parkour or Combat) until you reach the central hub. Collect any minor chests found in hidden corners for extra currency.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Locate the Puzzle Cubes">
    Find the two glowing cubes hidden within the side chambers. One cube represents the "Angel" and the other the "Demon."
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Match the Altars">
    Carry the cubes to the matching altars. The Angel cube must go to the white pedestal, and the Demon cube to the black pedestal.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Enter the Boss Portal">
    Once both cubes are placed, the barrier to the final arena will dissolve, allowing access to the Bone Dragon.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Puzzle Mechanic Info">
In group play, one player can carry a cube while others provide cover. Carrying a cube prevents you from using most combat skills, so ensure the path is clear of Scorpions before picking it up.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Boss Strategy: Defeating the Bone Dragon">

The final encounter at the **grand alfheim temple of beginnings location** is the Bone Dragon. This boss is designed to teach players about telegraphs and stamina management. It does not have the complex multi-phase mechanics of endgame bosses like those in Ode to the Abyss, but it can easily one-shot a level 10 player who ignores its animations.

| Attack Name | Visual Telegraph | Effect | Counter-Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bone Breath** | Dragon head tilts back | Linear AoE damage | Dash (Q) to the side immediately |
| **Tail Sweep** | Dragon pivots its body | 360-degree knockback | Jump or Block (F) if timing is right |
| **Ground Slam** | Front legs lift high | High impact stun | Back away or use invincibility frames |
| **Summon Minions** | Dragon roars upward | Spawns small skeletons | Use area-of-effect (AoE) class skills |

Before engaging, ensure your equipment is repaired at a blacksmith. The Treant miniboss, which appears earlier in the dungeon, acts as a "gear check." If you struggle to defeat the Treant, you likely need to upgrade your starter weapon to an Iron or Bone variant.

<Callout type="warning" title="Important Warning">
Do not exhaust all your stamina on a single M1 combo. The Bone Dragon often counters immediately after its breath attack. If you are out of stamina, you will be unable to dash away from the follow-up tail sweep.
</Callout>

</Section>
