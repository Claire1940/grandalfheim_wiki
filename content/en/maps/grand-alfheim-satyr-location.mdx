<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and Boss Patterns">

The Satyr is designed to test a player's patience and defensive timing. Unlike the chaotic patterns found in the Temple of Beginnings, the Satyr utilizes "committed" attacks. This means once the boss begins an animation, it cannot easily cancel it, leaving it vulnerable to counterattacks if you successfully dodge or block. Understanding these windows is the key to minimizing health loss during the encounter.

The boss primarily relies on heavy melee strikes. It will often lead with a series of quick swings followed by a massive overhead smash. If you are caught in the initial combo, your stamina will drain rapidly if you attempt to block the entire string. Instead, most veteran players recommend blocking the first hit to stabilize and then dashing (Q key) away from the follow-up heavy attacks.

<Section icon={Target} title="Preparation and Best Class Builds">

Before heading to the Salamander territory, you must ensure your character build is optimized for a sustained melee fight. While any class can technically defeat the Satyr, certain weapon styles have a distinct advantage due to their defensive capabilities or attack speed. Classes that rely on heavy, slow swings—like the Titanblade—may find the Satyr difficult to punish because the boss recovers faster than the player can complete a Greatsword animation.

Balanced classes like the **Swordsman** or high-mobility classes like the **Duelist** are excellent for this fight. The Swordsman provides a steady block and reliable M1 strings, while the Duelist can use its fast footwork to weave in and out of the Satyr's reach. If you are playing in a group, having an **Aegis** player to draw aggro with a Greatshield allows ranged classes like the **Bowmaster** to deal damage safely from a distance.
