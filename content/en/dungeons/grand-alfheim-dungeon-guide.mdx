<Section icon={Swords} title="The Temple of Beginnings: Step-by-Step Walkthrough">

The **Temple of Beginnings** is the first major hurdle for every adventurer. Accessible starting at level 10, this dungeon introduces the core mechanics you will face throughout your journey. The entrance is not fixed to a single spot; you can find portals within the **Salamander**, **Undine**, and **Gnome** territories, making it accessible regardless of your chosen race's starting zone.

Upon entering, players are presented with a choice that defines their dungeon experience: the **Parkour Route** or the **Combat Route**.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Choose Your Path">
    The left path focuses on **Parkour**. It requires precise jumping and flight management to avoid environmental hazards. The right path is the **Monster Route**, filled with Scorpions, Dinosaurs, and Treants. Groups looking for experience should take the right, while speedrunners prefer the left.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Cube Puzzle">
    Regardless of the route, both paths converge at the **Puzzle Rooms**. You must locate glowing cubes and place them on the matching **Angel and Demon altars**. The boss portal will remain sealed until all cubes are correctly seated.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Miniboss: The Treant">
    Before the final arena, you will encounter the **Treant**. This boss uses heavy, slow close-range attacks. The key here is **stamina management**. Do not spam M1 attacks; instead, wait for the Treant to swing, parry the hit, and then unleash your class skills.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Final Boss: Bone Dragon">
    The Bone Dragon is a high-mobility threat. Stay outside its direct line of fire. When it prepares its breath attack, dash to the side (Q) and follow up with a **Dash Attack** (Q+M1). Repeat this cycle until the dragon is grounded.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Environmental Hazards">
In the Parkour Route, falling into the void doesn't just reset your position; it drains a significant portion of your health. If your race wings are on cooldown, wait for the recharge before attempting long jumps.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Endgame Excellence: Ode to the Abyss Guide">

For those who have reached the level 50 cap, the **Ode to the Abyss** represents the pinnacle of current content. This dungeon is unique because it operates on a strict schedule. The chained entrance in the **Gnome territory** only opens at **12:00 AM and 12:00 PM Eastern Time**. Once the window opens, you have exactly 10 minutes to enter before it locks for another 12 hours.

The Abyss is not just a test of strength, but a test of coordination. You will descend via a massive elevator to reach **Valdrayn**, the dungeon's final protector.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="The Tower Mechanic">
    During Valdrayn's low-health phase, **four towers** will activate around the arena. The boss becomes invulnerable during this time. The party must split up to destroy all four towers simultaneously to break the shield.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Role Assignment">
    Assign your **Aegis** players to draw Valdrayn's aggro away from the towers. **Bowmasters** and **Mages** should focus on long-range tower destruction, while **Assassins** handle any adds that spawn.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Mythical Rewards">
    Clearing the Abyss is the only way to obtain **Level 50 Mythical equipment**. These items, such as the **Voidrune Edge**, offer the highest base stats and unique passive abilities in the game.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Boss Mechanics and Combat Tactics">

Mastering the **grand alfheim dungeon guide** requires more than just knowing where to go; you must master the combat system. Grand Alfheim uses a dynamic action-combat engine where timing is everything. 

**Core Combat Inputs for Dungeons:**
- **M1 (Left Click)**: Basic combo. Do not finish the full string if the boss is mid-animation.
- **F (Block/Parry)**: The most important button. A perfect parry stuns minibosses like the Satyr.
- **Q (Dash)**: Use this to escape AOE (Area of Effect) circles.
- **R (Critical Attack)**: Only use when the boss is in a "recovery" state after a missed heavy swing.
