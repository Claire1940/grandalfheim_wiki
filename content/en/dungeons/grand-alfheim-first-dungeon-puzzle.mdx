<Section icon={Swords} title="Choosing Your Dungeon Route">

Upon entering the Temple of Beginnings, your party is presented with a choice between two distinct paths. Both routes lead to the central puzzle chambers, but they require entirely different skill sets to complete. 

### The Left Route: Parkour Challenges
The left path is designed for players who prefer movement mechanics over raw combat. This route features vertical platforming challenges that test your timing, jumping, and flight control. Selecting this path allows you to bypass several large groups of enemies, making it the fastest route for experienced players.

### The Right Route: Combat Trials
The right path is a traditional dungeon crawl filled with hostile monsters. You will face waves of Scorpions, Dinosaurs, and aggressive plant-like enemies. This route is highly recommended for newer players because it provides valuable combat experience, class skill progression, and extra materials.

| Route | Primary Challenge | Enemy Density | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Left Path** | Vertical Platforming / Parkour | Very Low | Fast runs, experienced players |
| **Right Path** | Mob Clearing / Stamina Control | High | Leveling, class practice, loot |

<Callout type="tip" title="Solo vs Group Play">
While the left parkour route can be completed solo, the right combat route is much safer with a coordinated group containing an Aegis tank or an Undine healer.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Solving the Cube Puzzle">

The core obstacle of the Temple of Beginnings is the **grand alfheim first dungeon puzzle**. To open the portal leading to the final boss, your party must locate and place heavy stone cubes onto their corresponding altars. 

This puzzle requires coordination, as carrying a cube restricts your movement and prevents you from attacking.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate the Cubes">
    Search the central chambers of the temple to find the glowing stone cubes. There are two distinct types: Angel Cubes and Demon Cubes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Clear Surrounding Enemies">
    Before picking up a cube, eliminate all nearby temple guardians. Carrying a cube disables your weapons, leaving you completely defenseless.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Match Cubes to Altars">
    Carry the Angel Cube to the white Angel Altar, and transport the Demon Cube to the dark Demon Altar. Dropping a cube in the wrong spot resets its location.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Activate the Portal">
    Once both cubes are correctly placed on their respective altars, the central barrier will dissolve, revealing the portal to the Bone Dragon.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Puzzle Complete">
If a cube glitched or fell out of bounds during transport, exit the immediate puzzle room for 30 seconds to force the cubes to respawn at their starting points.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Defeating the Bosses and Loot Table">

The Temple of Beginnings features two major boss encounters: the Treant miniboss and the final Bone Dragon. Defeating these bosses is the primary method for obtaining high-tier mid-game equipment.

### Treant Miniboss
The Treant uses slow, heavy, close-range sweeping attacks. The key to this fight is timing your blocks and parries. Do not exhaust your stamina bar on offensive combos; always save enough stamina to perform a dodge roll or block when the Treant raises its arms.

### Bone Dragon Final Boss
The Bone Dragon fights inside a large circular arena. It utilizes devastating area-of-effect (AoE) breath attacks and tail swipes. Players should spread out around the arena to prevent the boss from targeting the entire party with a single breath attack. Punish the boss during its long recovery animations after it lands.

| Boss | Notable Attacks | Key Strategy | Highlight Drops |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Treant** | Close-range sweep, ground slam | Parry heavy swings, punish recovery | Snarl Hound Claws, Verdant Armor |
| **Bone Dragon** | Fire breath, tail swipe, aerial dive | Spread out, dodge AoE circles | Night's Edge, Obsidian Requiem |
