If you choose the Monster Path, be prepared for wave-based combat. The Scorpions and Dinosaurs encountered here have predictable attack patterns, but they often attack in clusters. Utilizing weapons with wide swings or area-of-effect (AoE) abilities is highly recommended to clear these rooms efficiently.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Defeating the Stump Ent and Elite Mobs">

Deep within the labyrinth of the **grand alfheim temple of beginnings**, you will encounter the Stump Ent. This enemy serves as a "skill check" for players, forcing them to learn the game's parry mechanics. The Stump Ent hits hard and can easily deplete the health of an unprepared adventurer in just a few strikes.

| Enemy Type | Primary Attack | Strategy | Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scorpion** | Rapid stings | Block and counter | Easy |
| **Dinosaur** | Heavy lunges | Side-dodge or parry | Medium |
| **Stump Ent** | Heavy slams | Perfect parry required | Hard |
| **Bone Dragon** | Breath & Tail | Stay behind/underneath | Boss |

<Callout type="success" title="Stump Ent Strategy">
The Stump Ent's pathfinding is limited to a straight line. If you are struggling with the parry timing, you can "cheese" the fight by keeping a wall or pillar between yourself and the Ent. It will often get stuck, allowing you to land safe hits.
</Callout>

Successfully navigating these encounters requires a balance of aggression and defense. In 2026, the meta for this dungeon involves using high-impact weapons that can stagger the Stump Ent, providing a window for high-damage combos.

This section requires players to interact with two separate rooms—the Angel Room (Green) and the Demon Room (Red). To progress to the boss, you must place specific boxes on their corresponding altars.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Boss Battle: The Bone Dragon">

The final challenge of the **grand alfheim temple of beginnings** is the Bone Dragon. This boss is a massive skeletal entity that utilizes a combination of physical tail swipes and elemental breath attacks. Unlike the smaller mobs earlier in the dungeon, the Bone Dragon requires constant movement and awareness of its "telegraphs" (visual cues before an attack).

**Key Mechanics to Watch:**
- **Tail Swipe**: A wide 180-degree arc behind the dragon. Avoid standing directly behind it for too long.
- **Breath Attack**: The dragon breathes a cone of energy in front of it. When it raises its head and glows, dash to its side.
- **Stomp**: A close-range AoE. If the dragon rears up on its hind legs, back away immediately.

| Attack Name | Warning Sign | Avoidance Maneuver |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Skeletal Breath** | Glowing chest/neck | Dash to the left or right |
| **Bone Crush** | Raising front legs | Jump or back-step |
| **Tail Whip** | Looking over shoulder | Move toward the neck |

<Callout type="warning" title="Boss Arena Hazard">
The arena is smaller than it looks. Be careful not to back yourself into a corner, as the Bone Dragon's large hitbox can "pin" you against the wall, making it impossible to dodge the breath attack.
</Callout>

Focus your damage on the dragon's legs to occasionally trip it, providing a 5-second window where it takes increased damage. High-DPS players can usually finish this fight in under three minutes if they maintain a consistent rhythm of dodging and attacking.

</Section>
